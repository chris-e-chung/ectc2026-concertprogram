SteamPunk'd

comp. Rannoch Purcell
Odaiko New England: Junko Kobayashi, Jasmine Hall, Brian Forbis, Marc Verhagen, Kumiko Yamamoto, Marly Coe, Amanda Ruiz, Megan Wu, Tomo Deeney, Kai Kishimoto

Rannoch Purcell was introduced to Steam Punk culture by his nephew and daughter. SteamPunk'd is composed around the idea of different components, big and small gears, clocks, metal parts working together like a machine. Rannoch Purcell (Razz) lived and taught taiko in Germany and founded Taiko Zentrum Duetschland, produced and hosted Taikopalooza, then returned to Utah and created UTAIKO and a taiko school in Utah.

Tatsumaki, or “whirlwind,” is an original composition by Hiroshi Tanaka, a former performing member of Taiko Center of the Pacific. The popular piece is played by taiko players all across the world and is a staple among collegiate groups in North America.

Pearl (2004; revised 2025-26 for Swarthmore Taiko Ensemble)

comp. Ian Cleworth
Swarthmore Taiko Ensemble: Evan Chin, Kay Evans, Laura Huang, Rei Kawakami, Kieran Mulligan, Bonji Onuma, Drake Roth, Max Saito-Rosenberg, Jennie Williams, Joe Small

A note from the composer, Ian Cleworth:

I first arrived in Tokyo on Japan’s National Culture Day in late 1981 and immediately made my way to Yoyogi Park. There, wide-eyed with wonder, I encountered demonstrations of culturally significant arts such as Judo, Odori (traditional dance), and Yabusame – a spectacular exhibition of horseback archery. However, having not yet heard taiko performed live – my only experience of it at that point being through LPs – you can imagine my excitement when, from some distance, the immense boom of Meiji Shrine’s Ōdaiko (grand drum) reached my ears. I ran as fast as I could in the hope of witnessing this imposing instrument being struck with such force, only to miss it by seconds.

Years later, I did eventually see the Ōdaiko played by one of the shrine priests, but the visceral impact of that first, distant encounter remains unmatched, and the power of its resonance continues to reverberate within me to this day.

Emperor Meiji’s taiko resounds far and wide, and I felt this was an appropriate aural image to draw upon when composing Pearl for Taikoz’s sister ensemble, Synergy Percussion, on the occasion of their 30th anniversary in 2004. Taikoz grew out of Synergy’s early explorations into taiko territory, and the resonance of those first concerts continues to be felt today.

As for the title? Well, what gift does one give for a 30th anniversary? Pearls, of course!

Minnasong

comp. Shoji Kameda, Eri Uchida
Shoji Kameda, Emily Harada, Elias Chanteloup, Bennett Elder, Grace Gu, Haojia Wang, Ryan Lowery, Oliver Leung, Karin Sannomiya, Kelvin Ng, Tammy Wilcox, Victoria Shao, Thomas Griebel

Minnasong is a groundbreaking new piece co-composed by Shoji Kameda and Eri Uchida designed with a simple, powerful philosophy: there is a place for everyone in taiko. True to its name, Minnasong (Everyone’s Song) features a wide range of parts, from accessible lines for newer players to challenging passages that will engage even the most seasoned performers. The piece incorporates katsugi okedo, nagado, shime, and okejime, creating a rich, orchestral tapestry.

East Coast Memories

comp. Patrick Cruz
North American Taiko Collective: Emily Harada, Sophia K Wang, Linda Liu, Yumi Hwang, Seith Hwang, Sonna Hwang. Led by Patrick Cruz.

Transposed directly from Patrick’s Taikai winning Solo ‘Letter To My Childhood’ this very special composition was specifically arranged to be performed at East Coast Taiko Conference. With the theme of Many Hearts, One Beat; this is the first time we will be performing together ever. Each telling the vulnerable story of what taiko means to us.

Zutto Futari de

comp. Michelle Fujii, Toru Watanabe
Unit Souzou: Michelle Fujii, Toru Watanabe

Zutto Futari de (Stuck Together) by Michelle Fujii & Toru Watanabe 2 people. 2 drums. Zutto Futari de (Stuck Together) negotiates the space between performer and drum tangling, yielding and synchronizing with each other. It acknowledges the complexity of 2 people building a relationship informed by their personal stories.
